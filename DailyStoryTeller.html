Daily Story Teller App

Children often consume content passively through videos or pre-made stories. Daily Story Teller flips that experience by inviting them to participate in storytelling through fun prompts, colorful design, and interactive choices. The goal was to create a safe, engaging, and educational space that nurtures creativity, language development, and imagination.

Daily Story Teller - Case Study

Case Study

The Challenge

Most kids' apps promote passive content consumption (videos, cartoons, audiobooks). Children rarely get opportunities to create, express, or imagine. Parents struggle to find safe digital spaces that encourage creativity.

There is a need for an app that enables kids to imagine, create, and share stories in a guided and safe environment.

Goals & Success Metrics

Project Goals

  • Encourage kids to create stories instead of just consuming content
  • Build a simple, safe, kid-friendly storytelling platform
  • Improve creativity, vocabulary, and daily learning habit

Success Indicators

  • Daily engagement & streak building
  • Number of stories created weekly
  • Kids completing at least 10 stories/month

Define

Problem Summary

Children spend most of their screen time passively consuming content (videos, games, and cartoons) rather than creating or expressing themselves. Parents struggle to find safe, educational platforms that encourage creativity and habit-building. There is a need for an engaging and guided storytelling experience that helps kids create, learn, and build confidence.

Opportunity: Transform passive screen time into an active, creative learning experience for children through guided and interactive storytelling.

How Might We?

  • How might we make storytelling fun, guided, and interactive for kids?
  • How might we encourage children to build a daily creative habit?
  • How might we give parents confidence that their child's screen time is safe, educational, and meaningful?

User Pain Points

  • Most apps encourage passive watching rather than creation.
  • Kids often lose interest in long or text-heavy activities.
  • Parents lack safe & ad-free learning platforms.
  • Limited tools for kids to express creativity independently.

User Needs

  • Guided prompts and visual storytelling support.
  • Interactive elements: voice, images, stickers & characters.
  • Rewards and progress streaks for motivation.
  • Safe environment with parental controls.

Ideation

Brainstorming & Crazy 8s

I ran a quick divergent session (20 mins) and a Crazy-8s sketch round to explore ways to turn passive screen time into creative storytelling. Ideas were grouped by creation method (voice / visual / text) and motivation (rewards / streaks).

Concept Shortlist

C1 — Guided Prompts

Daily prompt cards with visual cues help kids start quickly and reduce blank-page anxiety.

Pros: fast start, repeatable • Cons: needs variety
C2 — Voice-to-Story

Kids narrate; app transcribes and builds a simple storyboard with stickers and scenes.

Pros: accessible for younger kids • Cons: noisy env. issues
C3 — Build-a-Story (Cards)

Drag-and-drop character, setting, and plot cards to compose a story path.

Pros: playful, visual • Cons: needs curated packs

Why these concepts?

Mapped against research needs (fun, guided, low-friction, parental safety), these concepts balance ease of creation (prompts & voice) with playful control (cards), supporting habit building.

Key Solution Features (shortlist):
  • Daily guided prompts + templates
  • Voice narration → auto text + stickers
  • Drag-and-drop story cards (character / setting / plot)
  • Rewards, badges, and streaks for motivation
  • Parent dashboard with review & controls

Information Architecture & User Flow

Based on the defined needs, I mapped the core flow to ensure children can start creating a story with minimal friction. The focus was on simplicity (3–5 steps), clear guidance, and reward-based completion to encourage a creative routine.

Primary User Flow — Create a Story

  1. Open App → Select "Start Story"
  2. Choose Format: Write / Voice / Visual Cards
  3. Guided Prompts + Create Story
  4. Review & Edit
  5. Save → Earn Reward or Streak
  6. (Optional) Share with Parent Approval

Final Outcome

The app flow enables kids to quickly start creating with guidance, while parents get safety and oversight.

  • Focus on guided creation over passive consumption resonates with kids.
  • Small interaction tweaks (font size, fewer steps) drive completion.
  • Designing for kids requires clarity, feedback, and delight in every step.
